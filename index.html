🔧 Machine Logic & Obsolescence Guide

Confused about whether a new machine replaces an old one? This section clarifies every machine interaction. ✅ Core = always needed. 🔗 Useful = needed for recipes. ⚠️ Situational = use case dependent. ❌ Trap = never buy.

⚠️ Key Principle: Very few machines become obsolete! Most machines serve different recipe chains rather than replacing each other. The biggest mistake players make is thinking a new machine replaces an older one, when actually both are needed for different end-game products.

⛏️ Ore Processing (Pre-Smelting)

Ore Cleaner ✅ Core

Adds $10 to uncleaned ores. Always apply before smelting — the $10 compounds through ALL subsequent multipliers (smelt ×1.2, temper ×2, etc.).

Feeds: Polisher → Smelter | Obsolete? Never. Always needed.

Polisher ✅ Core

Adds $10 to any unpolished item. Used on ores (before smelting) AND gems (before cutting). Two completely different uses!

Feeds: Ores → Smelter, Gems → Gem Cutter | Obsolete? Never. Dual purpose.

🔥 Smelting

Ore Smelter ✅ Core

Ore → Bar (×1.2 value) + Stone byproduct. The stone is where gems and concrete bricks come from — hugely valuable.

Feeds: Bar → Tempering/Coiler/Plate/Bolts, Stone → Prospectors | Obsolete? Never.

Blast Furnace ❌ Trap

Ore → Bar (×0.9 value) + Stone. Costs $25,000 but makes bars 10% LESS valuable than the $1,500 Ore Smelter. There is NO scenario where this is better.

⛔ NEVER BUY. Ore Smelter is strictly superior in every way.

🔩 Bar Processing — Do I Need Both Coiler AND Plate Stamper?

💡 YES — both are needed! Coiler and Plate Stamper both add $20 to bars, which often confuses players. But they serve completely different recipe chains. Neither makes the other obsolete.

Coiler 🔗 Useful

Bar → Coil (+$20). Best early-game product. Later, Coils feed into Circuits (×2.0), Rings (×1.75), and Electromagnets (×1.5).

Feeds into: Circuits, Rings, Electromagnets | Obsolete? No! Needed for Tablets (via Circuit) and Power Cores (via Electromagnet).

Plate Stamper 🔗 Useful

Bar → Plate (+$20). Unlocks the entire metalwork chain: Pipes, Mech Parts, Filigree, and Casings (via Frames).

Feeds into: Pipes, Mech Parts, Filigree, Frames → Casings | Obsolete? No! Foundation of the entire main-line progression.

Bolt Machine 🔗 Useful

Bar → Bolts (+$15). Only $3K. Needed for Frames, which are needed for Casings, which are needed for EVERYTHING end-game.

Feeds into: Frame Maker → Casing Machine | Obsolete? No! Critical for all casing-based products.

Tempering Forge ✅ Core

Doubles untempered bar value (×2). This is NOT a replacement for smelting — it goes AFTER the smelter. Apply to bars before making Plates/Coils/Bolts.

Fed by: Ore Smelter (bar output) | Obsolete? Never. Massive multiplier.
✅ Does NOT replace Ore Smelter — you still smelt first, THEN temper.

🔧 Plate Products — Does Mech Parts Make Pipes Obsolete?

💡 NO! Mech Parts add more per plate ($30 vs $20), so they're better for direct selling. But Pipes are needed for Engines and Optics. Keep both.

Pipe Maker 🔗 Useful

Plate → Pipe (+$20). Lower value-add than Mech Parts, but Pipes are a required input for Engines (×1.2) and Optics (×1.25, for Lasers).

Feeds into: Engines, Optics → Lasers | Obsolete? No! Required for Engine and Laser recipes.

Mech Parts Maker 🔗 Useful

Plate → Mech Parts (+$30). Best $/plate for direct selling. Also a required input for Engines (×1.2).

Feeds into: Engines | Obsolete? No! Needed for Engine recipe alongside Pipes.

Filigree Cutter ⚠️ Situational

Plate → Filigree (+20% value). Only useful if you're making Gliders (Filigree + Amulet = ×1.5). Otherwise, Mech Parts give more per plate.

Feeds into: Gliders | Obsolete? N/A — niche use for Glider recipes only.

🧱 Stone Processing — Do Concrete Bricks Replace Fired Bricks?

💡 MOSTLY YES — Concrete bricks ($360/pair) are 5× more valuable than two fired bricks ($72/pair). Once you have the Cement Mixer, you should always prefer concrete bricks. But you still need fired bricks for odd surviving stones that can't be paired, and the Kiln is still needed for concrete brick processing.

Crusher ✅ Core

Anything → Dust ($25 set value). Required for bricks, cement, clay, glass, rings, and blasting powder. Literally everything in stone processing starts here.

Feeds into: Everything in stone processing | Obsolete? Never.

Brick Mold ✅ Core

Dust → Brick ($30) OR Cement → Concrete Brick ($300). Same machine, two inputs. You need it for BOTH regular and concrete bricks.

Fed by: Crusher (dust) or Cement Mixer (cement) | Obsolete? Never. Does double duty.

Kiln ✅ Core

Bricks → Fired Bricks (×1.2) AND Dust → Glass ($30). Still needed after Cement Mixer because concrete bricks also pass through the Kiln for the final boost.

Feeds into: Glass → Circuits/Tablets, Fired/Concrete Bricks → Seller | Obsolete? Never.
✅ Cement Mixer does NOT replace the Kiln — concrete bricks still need to be kilned.

Cement Mixer ✅ Core

Dust + Stone → Cement ($50). The cement goes to Brick Mold to make concrete bricks ($300). Biggest single upgrade in the game.

Fed by: Crusher + raw stone | Feeds: Brick Mold → Kiln → Seller
✅ Makes fired-only bricks MOSTLY obsolete (5× value per stone pair), but doesn't replace Crusher, Brick Mold, or Kiln.

Clay Mixer ⚠️ Situational

2 Dust → Clay Block ($50). Only useful as input for Ceramic Furnace → Superconductors. Don't confuse with Cement Mixer!

Feeds into: Ceramic Furnace → Superconductors | Obsolete? N/A — specific to SC recipe.

Ceramic Furnace ⚠️ Situational

Clay Block → Ceramic Casing ($150). Only useful as a component for Superconductors.

Feeds into: Superconductor Constructor | Obsolete? N/A — specific to SC recipe.

🔄 Sifting & Recycling

Sifter ⚠️ Situational

10% chance: Dust → random low-tier ore (Tin, Iron, Cobalt, etc.). Dust is NOT destroyed on failure. Free bonus ore, but low success rate.

Fed by: Crusher (dust) | Feeds: Sifted ore → back to main line
⚠️ Becomes fully obsolete when you get the Nano Sifter (prestige). Replace immediately.

Nano Sifter ⭐ Prestige

16.6% sift rate (vs 10%) AND produces rarer ores (up to Iridium/Palladium). Strictly superior replacement for the regular Sifter.

Replaces: Sifter | Cost: 1 Medal (cheapest prestige machine)
✅ Makes regular Sifter completely obsolete. Get this FIRST after prestige.

💎 Gem Machines — Do They Replace Each Other?

💡 NO — Prospectors STACK! Each prospector checks the stone independently (5% each). Running a stone through all 5 prospectors gives a 22.6% cumulative chance of getting a gem. They don't compete — they cooperate. Buy them all.

Topaz→Diamond Prospectors ✅ Core

Each has a 5% independent chance to convert a stone into its gem. They STACK — a stone passes through all of them sequentially. 22.6% cumulative chance with all 5.

Fed by: Smelter (stone byproduct) | Feeds: Gems → Polisher → Gem Cutter
✅ None replaces any other. Buy all 5 as soon as you can afford them.

Gem Cutter ✅ Core

Polished Gem → Cut Gem (×1.4). A multiplier on ALL prospector output. Must Polish first, then Cut. Apply to every single gem before selling or using in recipes.

Fed by: Polisher (gems only) | Feeds: Seller, or Prismatic Gem Crucible

Prismatic Gem Crucible ⚠️ Situational

2 Cut Gems → Prismatic Gem (×1.25 combined value). Only worth it if you're making Amulets or Lasers. For direct selling, it's only +25% on TWO gems.

Feeds into: Amulets, Lasers | Worth it? Only for end-game gem recipes.

🔗 Combination Machines (Multi-Input)

💡 These machines COMBINE items — they don't replace anything. Each requires specific inputs from earlier machines. Think of them as "assembly" steps, not upgrades.

Frame Maker 🔗 Useful

Bar + Bolts → Frame (×1.25 combined). Required for Casings, Amulets.

Needs: Smelter + Bolt Machine | Feeds: Casing Machine, Amulet Maker

Casing Machine ✅ Core

Frame + Bolts + Plate → Casing (×1.30). Used in Power Cores, Tablets, Engines, Electromagnets, Explosives. Most-used combination machine.

Needs: 4 ores worth of tempered bars | Feeds: Everything end-game

Circuit Maker 🔗 Useful

Glass + Coil → Circuit (×2.0 combined). Great multiplier. Required for Tablets and Lasers.

Needs: Kiln (glass) + Coiler | Feeds: Tablet Factory, Laser Maker

Alloy Furnace 🔗 Useful

2 Bars → Alloy Bar (×1.2 combined). Only used for Superconductors. Does NOT replace single bars — you still need those for Plates/Coils/Bolts.

Needs: 2 tempered bars | Feeds: Superconductor Constructor
✅ Does NOT replace Ore Smelter or Tempering Forge. It combines their outputs.

Magnetic Machine 🔗 Useful

Coil + Metal Casing → Electromagnet (×1.5 combined). Required for Power Cores.

Needs: Coiler + Casing Machine | Feeds: Power Core Assembler

Ring Maker ⚠️ Situational

Metal Dust + Coil → Ring (×1.75). Only for Amulet recipes. Requires metal dust (from crushing a bar).

Needs: Crusher (on bar) + Coiler | Feeds: Amulet Maker

🏆 End-Game Assemblers

Power Core Assembler ✅ Core

Casing + Superconductor + Electromagnet → Power Core (×3.5). Best $/ore in the game. Requires the most complex factory setup.

Needs: 11 ores worth of inputs | Best product in the game

Tablet Factory 🔗 Useful

Casing + Glass + Circuit → Tablet (×3.0). 2nd best $/ore. Add Electronic Tuner (+$50) before QA for max value.

Needs: Casing + Glass (Kiln) + Circuit (Coil+Glass) | 5 ores total

Engine Factory 🔗 Useful

Mech Parts + Pipe + Casing → Engine (×1.2). Decent $/ore but lower multiplier than Tablet/PC.

Needs: Mech Parts + Pipe + Casing | 6 ores total

Superconductor Constructor 🔗 Useful

Alloy Bar + Ceramic Casing → Superconductor (×3.0). Great multiplier, but mainly used as Power Core input.

Needs: Alloy Furnace + Ceramic Furnace | 2 ores + 2 stones

Laser Maker ⚠️ Situational

Optics + Gem + Circuit → Laser (×2.75). Highest gem multiplier, but requires many components. Only worth it with high-value gems.

Needs: Optics (Lens+Pipe) + Cut Gem + Circuit | Complex setup

Quality Assurance Machine ✅ Core

Adds 20% to untested items. Always apply last — to your most expensive finished product. Items can only be QA'd once.

Feeds: Seller | Obsolete? Never. Final multiplier on everything.

⭐ Prestige Machines

Prestige machines are unlocked with Medals earned by reaching $20M+ before resetting. They are kept permanently across all future prestiges. Data from the UMT Wiki.

⚠️ Prestige resets: Money, factory machines, vehicles, and most equipment. You keep cosmetics, Robux purchases, and all Medal-bought prestige machines. First prestige requires $20,000,000.

⭐ Philosopher's Stone — 3 Medals

Multiplies ore value by ×1.25 (25% boost). Works on ores only, once per ore, before any other processing.

📐 Size: 5×3 blocks | Apply BEFORE cleaning/polishing so the +25% compounds through all subsequent multipliers.

With Lead ($30): $30 × 1.25 = $37.50. That extra $7.50 compounds to +$18 per ore at the end of a full chain.

⭐ Nano Sifter — 1 Medal

Upgraded Sifter: 16.6% chance to produce an ore from dust (vs regular Sifter's 10%). Produces rarer ores including Iridium and Palladium.

📐 Size: 3×3 blocks | Cheapest prestige machine. Get this first for free recycling value from dust.

⭐ Duplicator — 8 Medals

Duplicates any item, but each copy is worth half value. Items can only be duplicated once. Derived items from duplicated inputs can't be duplicated again.

📐 Most expensive prestige machine. Best used to duplicate high-value ores/bars early in the chain. Since v0.5.4, duplicated dust returns to $1 value (not $0.5).

💡 Optimal Duplicator placement: Duplicate the ore BEFORE processing. You get 2 ores at half value each, but both go through the full processing chain separately. Net result: 2 items at 50% value each = 100% of original value, but doubled throughput through your factory — effectively 2× the output rate.

⭐ Gem→Bar Transmuter — 4 Medals

Converts gems into metal bars of equivalent value. The bar can then be processed through metalwork chains (temper, plate, casing, etc.).

Useful when gem chains are maxed but metalwork multipliers are higher. A Diamond ($1,500) becomes a $1,500 bar that can be tempered to $3,000!

⭐ Bar→Gem Transmuter — 4 Medals

Converts metal bars into gems of equivalent value. The gem can then go through jewelcrafting chains (cutting, rings, amulets, lasers).

Useful when you have surplus bars but need gems for Lasers or Amulets. A tempered Gold bar ($840) becomes an $840 gem for cutting!

⭐ Recommended Prestige Medal Priority
1st: Nano Sifter (1M) → 2nd: Ore Upgrader (3M) → 3rd: Phil. Stone (3M) → 4th: Transmuters (4M ea) → Last: Duplicator (8M)

Nano Sifter first — cheapest (1 medal), immediate recycling value from dust. Ore Upgrader next — massive ROI on lower-tier ores. Philosopher's Stone — guaranteed ×1.25 on every ore, compounds through all chains. Transmuters — unlock cross-chain flexibility late-game. Duplicator last — costs 8 medals but the half-value penalty limits early usefulness.

🔍 Filters

Route specific item types onto certain conveyor paths. Critical for separating gems from surviving stones after prospectors, and for routing different products in multi-line factories.

Use filters after prospectors to separate gems from surviving stones → different processing chains. Smelters already split bars and stones automatically.

🛗 Elevators

Move items vertically between factory levels. Your plot is 30×30 with 100 blocks height and 4,000 complexity.

Build vertically to save floor space. Stack processing chains on multiple levels.

🚧 Gate Belt

Conditional pass-through that controls item flow. Acts as a switch to allow or block items based on conditions.

Useful for managing overflow, recycling jams, or gating output to the Seller.

➡️ Conveyors ($10 each)

Basic horizontal transport between machines. Rotatable in Build Mode. The backbone of every factory layout.

Belt speed can be a bottleneck. Use parallel lines for high-throughput setups.

🏗️ Factory Layout Tips:
• Smelters split bars and stones automatically — no filter needed after smelting
• Filter after prospectors — separate gems from surviving stones for different processing
• Prospect stones before any other processing — use a separate line for the 5 prospectors
• Overflow splitters prevent jams — always have a "drain" path so items don't clog
• Build vertically — 100 block height means you can stack 3-4 full processing floors
• Upgrade unloader output belts — the #1 throughput bottleneck is usually the unloader

💡 Pro Tips & Key Insights

Critical optimization rules for maximum profit.

🔑 Rule #0: ALWAYS prospect stones before processing them.
With all 5 prospectors + Gem Cutter, each stone has an expected gem value of ~$141. This is FREE value — the stone is destroyed only if a gem is found (22.6% chance), and the gem is worth far more than what you'd make from the stone. Prospectors pay for themselves extremely fast.
🔑 Rule #1: Always Clean + Polish ores BEFORE smelting.
The $20 value added by cleaning+polishing gets multiplied by ALL subsequent operations (×1.2 smelt, ×2 temper, etc.). Early flat bonuses compound into massive gains.
🔑 Rule #2: Always QA your final products last.
The QA Machine adds 20% to any item's final value. Apply it to your most expensive finished products (Power Cores, Tablets, etc.) for maximum dollar gain.
🔑 Rule #3: Never sell raw stones.
Stones from smelting are worth massive value when prospected + processed. Raw stone = $0. Prospected + concrete bricked = $180+/stone plus ~$141 gem EV.
⚠️ The Blast Furnace is a trap!
It costs $25,000 but makes bars 10% LESS valuable (×0.9 vs Ore Smelter's ×1.2). The Ore Smelter is strictly better for $1,500.
💰 Diamond Prospector has the highest EV per stone.
Diamond's $1,500 base value (→ $2,114 cut) means the Diamond Prospector alone contributes ~$86 of the ~$141 total prospector EV per stone. It costs $30K but pays for itself in just ~350 stones. Buy it ASAP.
🔗 Why Plates over Coils for the main line?
Both add +$20 to bars. But plates unlock Pipes (+$20), Mech Parts (+$30), and Filigree (+20%). Coils feed into Circuits and Rings. The plate path has more branches and higher single-line value.
📊 The "Per Ore" Metric:
Always compare value per ore consumed, not total product value. Include prospector gem EV + concrete brick byproducts. Power Core: $1,614/ore. Tablet: $1,090/ore.
⭐ Prestige machines compound with EVERYTHING:
Ore Upgrader + Philosopher's Stone applied before cleaning/polishing means the extra value gets multiplied by smelt (×1.2), temper (×2), and ALL combination machines. A $30 Lead becomes a $62.50 effective ore, compounding to +65% per Power Core!
⭐ Nano Sifter > regular Sifter:
16.6% success rate vs 10%, AND it produces rarer ores (up to Iridium/Palladium). Replace your regular Sifter immediately after first prestige — it only costs 1 Medal.
