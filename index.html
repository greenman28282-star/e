📈 Stage-by-Stage Optimal Setups

Each stage includes prospectors in the stone byproduct flow. The Ore Smelter produces 1 stone per 2 ores — all per-ore totals include stone byproducts scaled by this rate. Investment costs are cumulative. Green = new this stage

Stage 1: Foundation + First Prospector
Investment: $6,210
Polisher $250 Ore Cleaner $80 Ore Smelter $380 Crusher $1,750 Coiler $1,750 Topaz Prospector $2,000

Start by maximizing bar value with cleaning + polishing before smelting. Coils give the best value-add at this price. The Topaz Prospector is only $2K — run every stone through it before crushing. 5% chance of a $75 Topaz from a worthless stone!

MAIN LINE:
Ore ($30) → Clean (+$10) → Polish (+$10) → Smelt (×1.2) → Coil (+$20) → 💰 Sell Coil ($80)
BYPRODUCT LINE (per stone):
Stone → 💎 Topaz Prosp. (5%) → Crush → Dust ($1) → Polish ($11) → 💰 Sell ($11)
↳ 5% chance: Topaz ($75) → Polish (+$10) → 💰 Sell ($85)
💎 Prospector EV per stone: $4.25 (5% × $85 polished Topaz)
Total per ore: $93 (Coil: $80 + 95% Dust: $23.75 + Gem EV: $4.25)
🚂 Transport: Simple linear layout. Conveyor from Unloader → Cleaner → Polisher → Smelter. The Smelter splits output automatically — bars go one direction (→ Coiler → Seller), stones go the other (→ Topaz Prospector). Use a Filter after the Prospector to separate gems (→ Polisher → Seller) from surviving stones (→ Crusher → Polisher → Seller). Note: stones didn't go through the ore Polisher, so a dedicated Polisher on the stone line adds +$10 to dust.

⭐ Stage 5: Concrete Revolution
Investment: $66,460
All previous Cement Mixer $10,000

Biggest single upgrade in the game. Cement Mixer lets you combine dust + stone into cement ($30), which the Brick Mold turns into concrete bricks ($100), which the Kiln boosts to $120. Polish adds another $10, and QA brings it to $156. That's $156 from 2 free stones! Stones that survive prospectors get paired up for concrete bricks.

🚀 Game-changer! Concrete bricks ($156 QA'd per pair) significantly outperform fired bricks ($48 QA'd each). Combined with 4 prospectors, your byproduct line now generates solid value.
MAIN LINE (per ore):
Ore → Clean → Polish → Smelt → Plate → Mech Parts → 💰 Sell ($110)
BYPRODUCT LINE (per stone):
Stone → 💎 4 Prospectors (18.55%) → Surviving Stone (81.45%)
Surviving stones pair up for concrete bricks:
Stone #1 (keep) + Stone #2 → Crush → Dust → Cement ($30) → Brick Mold ($100) → Kiln → Polish → 💰 $130
💎 Prospector EV per stone: $38.84 (4 prospectors — same as Stage 4)
Total per ore: $295 (Mech Parts: $110 + Concrete Brick share: $146.61 + Gem EV: $38.84)
📈 Concrete bricks + prospectors = massive byproduct income!

🚂 Transport (COMPLEX):
• After all 4 prospectors → Filter: gems go to Polisher → Seller, surviving stones continue

Stage 7: Casings, Circuits & Frames (End-Game Prep)
Investment: $257,760
All previous Bolt Machine $2,800 Electronic Tuner $8,500 Frame Maker $10,000 Circuit Maker $20,000 Casing Machine $50,000
✅ Keep your Stage 6 lines running! Your tempered mech parts line ($170/ore) and concrete brick byproduct line stay active. Casings are not a direct upgrade — they're a new parallel production chain that you build in addition to your existing setup. You'll stockpile casings now and use them in end-game recipes (Power Cores, Tablets, Engines) where their value multiplies dramatically.
🔗 Circuit Maker + Electronic Tuner: The Circuit Maker combines Glass (from Kiln) + Coil → Circuit (×2.0). Circuits are required for Tablets (2nd best product!) and Lasers. The Electronic Tuner adds a flat +$50 to Tablets before QA (+$60 effective after QA's ×1.2). Buy both now — you'll need them as soon as you unlock the Tablet Factory. Reactivate your Coiler from Stage 1 — it's needed again for circuits and electromagnets.

Metal casings are the foundation of end-game products. A casing needs 4 ores (1 bar + 1 bolt → frame, then frame + 1 bolt + 1 plate). The Circuit Maker prepares for Tablet production by combining glass + coils. Save casings and circuits for end-game recipes. ~2 stones from these 4 ores run through prospectors first (Smelter: 1 stone per 2 ores).

CASING RECIPE (4 ores → 1 casing + ~2 stones for prospecting):
Ore #1 → Tempered Bar ┐
Ore #2 → Tempered Bolts ┤ Frame Maker (×1.25) ┐
Ore #3 → Tempered Bolts ───────────────────────┤ Casing Machine (×1.30) → 💰 Casing ($607)
Ore #4 → Tempered Plate ───────────────────────┘
4 STONES → 5 Prospectors → Surviving → Concrete Bricks
💡 Why build casings now if mech parts are better $/ore? At ore value $30, selling tempered mech parts = $170/ore, while raw casings = only $152/ore. Don't sell casings raw — their value comes from being combined into end-game products (Power Cores, Tablets, Engines) where multipliers stack to $1,000+/ore. Build the casing line now so it's ready when you unlock those assemblers. In the meantime, your Stage 6 mech parts line keeps earning.
Casing value: $607 from 4 ores | Per ore (with prospected byproducts): $289
🚂 Transport (VERY COMPLEX):
• Bar routing: 4 ores smelt into 4 tempered bars. Use splitters to route bars: some to Bolt Machine, some to Plate Stamper, some to Coiler, and some pass through for Frame Maker input
• Frame Maker takes Bar + Bolt; Casing Machine takes Frame + Bolt + Plate — use Overflow Splitters to buffer components
• Circuit Maker: Route one surviving stone → Crusher → Kiln → Glass ($30), then combine with a Coil → Circuit (×2.0). Store circuits for Tablet production
• All smelter stone outputs → feed into prospector chain → Filter (gems → Polisher → Gem Cutter → Seller)
• Surviving stones → concrete brick pairing (L/R Splitter → Crusher/Cement → Brick Mold → Kiln → Polisher → Seller)
• Elevators highly recommended — put bar processing on Floor 1, stone/gem on Floor 2, assembly on Floor 3
• Consider multiple sellers: one for casings, one for gems, one for bricks

✅ What stays from earlier stages: Your Stage 6 mech parts line, Stage 7 casing + circuit lines all remain active. Stage 8 adds another parallel chain — alloy bars and ceramic casings — which combine into superconductors. You're still expanding, not replacing. Alloy bars merge 2 tempered bars (+20%). Ceramic casings from clay ($150). Superconductors combine alloy + ceramic for ×3 value! Stones that survive prospectors now split: some for ceramics, rest for concrete bricks.

SUPERCONDUCTOR (2 ores + 2 surviving stones for ceramic):
2 Ores → 2 Tempered Bars → Alloy Furnace (×1.2) ┐
2 Surviving Stones → Crush → 2 Dust → Clay ($50) → Ceramic ($150) ┤ Superconductor (×3.0) → 💰 ($1,498)
All stones from smelting pass through 5 prospectors first! Surviving stones used for ceramic or concrete bricks.
💎 Prospector EV per stone: $137.87 (5 prospectors + Polish + Cut — same as Stage 6)
⚡ Best immediate sell: Superconductors give $749/ore from the product (2 ores). Plus gem EV from ~1 prospected stone (0.5/ore): $138. Total per ore: $887. Note: the ceramic recipe requires 2 surviving stones — these come from extra ore smelting or stone reserves.
Superconductor: $1,498 from 2 ores | Per ore (with gem EV): $1,025
🚂 Transport:
• Alloy Furnace takes 2 tempered bars — use a L/R Splitter to alternate bars: one to Alloy, one to Coiler (for Electromagnet)
• All stones → 5 prospectors → Filter (gems → Polisher → Gem Cutter → Seller)
• For ceramic: 2 surviving stones → Crusher → Clay Mixer → Ceramic Furnace. Use a dedicated splitter to route exactly 2 stones per ceramic batch
• Remaining surviving stones → concrete brick pairing (Crusher → Cement Mixer → Brick Mold → Kiln → Polisher → Seller)
• SC Constructor takes Alloy + Ceramic — use Gate Belts to synchronize both inputs arriving together
• Three-floor layout: Floor 1: Bar processing + Alloy. Floor 2: Stone prospecting + ceramic/brick. Floor 3: Assembly (SC, Electromagnet, Power Core prep)

⭐ Prestige Machines

Prestige machines are unlocked with Medals earned by reaching $20M+ before resetting. They are kept permanently across all future prestiges. Data from the UMT Wiki.

⚠️ Prestige resets: Money, factory machines, vehicles, and most equipment. You keep cosmetics, Robux purchases, and all Medal-bought prestige machines. First prestige requires $20,000,000.

⭐ Philosopher's Stone — 3 Medals

Multiplies ore value by ×1.25 (25% boost). Works on ores only, once per ore, before any other processing.

📐 Size: 5×3 blocks | Apply BEFORE cleaning/polishing so the +25% compounds through all subsequent multipliers.

With your current ore ($30): the ×1.25 boost compounds to +$18 per ore at the end of a full tempered bar chain.

⭐ Nano Sifter — 1 Medal

Upgraded Sifter: 16.6% chance to produce an ore from dust (vs regular Sifter's 10%). Produces rarer ores including Iridium and Palladium.

📐 Size: 3×3 blocks | Cheapest prestige machine. Get this first for free recycling value from dust.

⭐ Duplicator — 8 Medals

Duplicates any item, but each copy is worth half value. Items can only be duplicated once. Derived items from duplicated inputs can't be duplicated again.

📐 Most expensive prestige machine. Best used to duplicate high-value ores/bars early in the chain. Since v0.5.4, duplicated dust returns to $1 value (not $0.5).

💡 Optimal Duplicator placement: Duplicate the ore BEFORE processing. You get 2 ores at half value each, but both go through the full processing chain separately. Net result: 2 items at 50% value each = 100% of original value, but doubled throughput through your factory — effectively 2× the output rate.

⭐ Gem→Bar Transmuter — 4 Medals

Converts gems into metal bars of equivalent value. The bar can then be processed through metalwork chains (temper, plate, casing, etc.).

Useful when gem chains are maxed but metalwork multipliers are higher. A Diamond ($1,500) becomes a $1,500 bar that can be tempered to $3,000!

⭐ Bar→Gem Transmuter — 4 Medals

Converts metal bars into gems of equivalent value. The gem can then go through jewelcrafting chains (cutting, rings, amulets, lasers).

Useful when you have surplus bars but need gems for Lasers or Amulets. A tempered Gold bar ($840) becomes an $840 gem for cutting!

⭐ Recommended Prestige Medal Priority
1st: Nano Sifter (1M) → 2nd: Ore Upgrader (3M) → 3rd: Phil. Stone (3M) → 4th: Transmuters (4M ea) → Last: Duplicator (8M)

Nano Sifter first — cheapest (1 medal), immediate recycling value from dust. Ore Upgrader next — massive ROI on lower-tier ores. Philosopher's Stone — guaranteed ×1.25 on every ore, compounds through all chains. Transmuters — unlock cross-chain flexibility late-game. Duplicator last — costs 8 medals but the half-value penalty limits early usefulness.

⏰ When to Prestige
🎯 First prestige: as soon as you hit $20M. Don't delay — the Nano Sifter (1 medal) immediately adds recycling value to every dust in your factory. Each subsequent prestige gets faster because your prestige machines carry over.

Prestige threshold: $20,000,000 cash to unlock the prestige button.
Medal calculation: You earn 1 Medal per prestige. The more money you have when you prestige, the more medals you can earn in later prestiges.
Strategy: Your first few prestiges should be fast — rush to $20M with a simple Tempered Mech Parts factory, prestige, repeat. Each time your prestige machines make the next run faster. Aim for Nano Sifter (1M) → Ore Upgrader (3M) → Philosopher's Stone (3M) in your first 7 medals.
When to stop prestiging: Once you have all the prestige machines you want, there's no further benefit to resetting. Focus on building your ultimate factory.
