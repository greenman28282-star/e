📈 Stage-by-Stage Optimal Setups

Progress through these stages as your budget grows. Each stage builds on the previous one.

Stage 1: Foundation
Investment: $7,100
Polisher $850 Ore Cleaner $1,000 Ore Smelter $1,500 Crusher $1,750 Coiler $1,750

Start by maximizing bar value with cleaning + polishing before smelting. Coils give the best single-step value-add for bars at this price. Crush stones into dust for later use.

MAIN LINE:
Ore ($10) → Clean (+$10) → Polish (+$10) → Smelt (×1.2) → Coil (+$20) → 💰 Sell Coil ($44)
BYPRODUCT LINE:
Stone (from smelt) → Crush → Dust ($25) → 💰 Sell Dust ($25)
Total per ore: $69 (Coil: $44 + Dust: $25)
Stage 2: Bricks & Pipes
Investment: $17,600
Polisher Ore Cleaner Ore Smelter Crusher Brick Mold $2,000 Plate Stamper $3,500 Pipe Maker $4,000

Switch from Coils to Plates→Pipes for more value. Turn dust into bricks. Keep the Coiler for later — coils will be critical in end-game circuits and rings.

MAIN LINE:
Ore → Clean → Polish → Smelt (×1.2) → Plate (+$20) → Pipe (+$20) → 💰 Sell Pipe ($64)
BYPRODUCT LINE:
Stone → Crush → Dust → Brick Mold ($30) → 💰 Sell Brick ($30)
Total per ore: $94 (Pipe: $64 + Brick: $30)
Stage 3: Kiln Upgrade
Investment: $22,350
Polisher Ore Cleaner Ore Smelter Crusher Brick Mold Plate Stamper Pipe Maker Kiln $4,750

The Kiln fires bricks for +20% value. A simple but effective boost to your byproduct line.

MAIN LINE: (same as Stage 2)
Ore → Clean → Polish → Smelt → Plate → Pipe → 💰 Sell Pipe ($64)
BYPRODUCT LINE:
Stone → Crush → Dust → Brick ($30) → Kiln (+20%) → 💰 Sell Fired Brick ($36)
Total per ore: $100 (Pipe: $64 + Fired Brick: $36)
Stage 4: Mechanical Parts
Investment: $28,350
All previous Mech Parts Maker $6,000

Mechanical Parts add $30 to plates vs Pipes' $20. Switch your main line to Mech Parts for an immediate $10/ore boost. Keep the Pipe Maker — you'll need pipes for end-game engines and optics.

MAIN LINE:
Ore → Clean → Polish → Smelt → Plate (+$20) → Mech Parts (+$30) → 💰 Sell ($74)
BYPRODUCT LINE: (same as Stage 3)
Stone → Crush → Brick → Kiln → 💰 Fired Brick ($36)
Total per ore: $110 (Mech Parts: $74 + Fired Brick: $36)
⭐ Stage 5: Concrete Revolution
Investment: $38,350
All previous Cement Mixer $10,000

This is the biggest single upgrade in the game. The Cement Mixer lets you combine dust + stone into cement ($50), which the Brick Mold turns into concrete bricks ($300), which the Kiln boosts to $360. That's $360 from two free stones — a 10× improvement over fired bricks!

🚀 Game-changer! The concrete brick path turns worthless stone byproducts into $360 each (per pair). This will be your primary money maker for several stages.
MAIN LINE (per ore):
Ore → Clean → Polish → Smelt → Plate → Mech Parts → 💰 Sell ($74)
BYPRODUCT LINE (per 2 stones from 2 ores):
Stone #1 (keep) + Stone #2 → Crush → Dust ($25) → Cement Mixer ($50) → Brick Mold ($300) → Kiln (+20%) → 💰 Sell ($360)
Total per ore: $254 (Mech Parts: $74 + Concrete Brick share: $180/ore)
📈 That's a +131% improvement over Stage 4!
Stage 6: Tempering
Investment: $88,350
All previous Tempering Forge $50,000

The Tempering Forge doubles bar value before any further processing. This is a massive multiplier that affects everything downstream. Clean→Polish→Smelt→Temper→process.

MAIN LINE:
Ore → Clean → Polish → Smelt → ⚡ Temper (×2) → Plate (+$20) → Mech Parts (+$30) → 💰 Sell ($98)
BYPRODUCT: Same concrete brick path ($360 per 2 stones)
Total per ore: $278 (Tempered Mech Parts: $98 + Concrete Brick share: $180)
Stage 7: Casings & Frames
Investment: $151,350
All previous Bolt Machine $3,000 Frame Maker $10,000 Casing Machine $50,000

Now you can build metal casings — the foundation of end-game products. A casing requires 4 ores worth of bars (1 bar + 1 bolt → frame, plus 1 bolt + 1 plate). The casing itself is very valuable, but the real power comes when you use it in end-game recipes.

CASING RECIPE (4 ores → 1 casing):
Ore #1 → Tempered Bar ┐
Ore #2 → Tempered Bolts ┤ Frame Maker (×1.25) ┐
Ore #3 → Tempered Bolts ───────────────────────┤ Casing Machine (×1.30) → 💰 Casing ($351)
Ore #4 → Tempered Plate ───────────────────────┘
💡 Strategy choice: At ore value $10, selling tempered mech parts + concrete bricks = $278/ore. Selling casings + concrete bricks = $268/ore. Mech parts are still slightly better for immediate selling! Build casings only when saving components for end-game products.
Casing value: $351 from 4 ores | Per ore (with concrete bricks): $268
Stage 8: Alloy & Superconductors
Investment: $321,350
All previous Clay Mixer $20,000 Ceramic Furnace $30,000 Alloy Furnace $100K Magnetic Machine $100K

Now you can make alloy bars (merge 2 tempered bars, +20%) and ceramic casings from stones (2 dust → clay → ceramic $150). Combine alloys + ceramics into superconductors (×3 value!).

SUPERCONDUCTOR (2 ores + 2 stones):
2 Ores → 2 Tempered Bars → Alloy Furnace (×1.2) ┐
2 Stones → Crush → 2 Dust → Clay ($50) → Ceramic ($150) ┤ Superconductor (×3.0) → 💰 ($968)
ELECTROMAGNET (5 ores):
1 Ore → Tempered Coil + 4 Ores → Casing → Magnetic Machine (×1.5) → 💰 ($875)
⚡ Best immediate sell at this stage: Superconductors give $484/ore (using stone byproducts for ceramics). This far surpasses selling mech parts ($278/ore).
Superconductor: $968 from 2 ores + 2 stones | Per ore: $484

💎 Gem Strategy

Gems come from prospecting stones (5% each) and sifting dust (10% for common gems). Since gem base values aren't specified in the README, here's the optimal processing strategy regardless of value.

Gem Prospecting Chain
Stone → Topaz (5%) → Emerald (5%) → Sapphire (5%) → Ruby (5%) → Diamond (5%) → Surviving Stone (77.4%)

Run every stone through all 5 prospectors before using it for anything else. The stone passes through each with a 95% survival rate. Overall: ~22.6% chance of becoming some gem.

💎 Gem Processing Priority:
1. Gem Cutter → Cut gems (+40% value)
2. Prismatic Gem Crucible → Merge 2 cut gems (+25% combined value)
3. Use prismatic gems in: Amulets (ring + frame + prismatic gem, ×2.0) or Lasers (optics + gem + circuit, ×2.75)
4. Filigree + Glider → Combine filigree with jewellery (×1.5) for extra value on amulets
🔮 Sifter Bonus: Always run dust through the Sifter before using it for bricks/glass/clay. The dust isn't destroyed, and you get a 10% chance of a free common gem ore. It's literally free value.

💡 Pro Tips & Key Insights

Critical optimization rules for maximum profit.

🔑 Rule #1: Always Clean + Polish ores BEFORE smelting.
The $20 value added by cleaning+polishing gets multiplied by ALL subsequent operations (×1.2 smelt, ×2 temper, etc.). Polishing a $10 ore adds $20, which becomes $48 after smelt+temper. Polishing a finished Power Core adds only $10. Early flat bonuses compound into massive gains.
🔑 Rule #2: Always QA your final products last.
The QA Machine adds 20% to any item's final value. Apply it to your most expensive finished products (Power Cores, Tablets, etc.) for maximum dollar gain.
🔑 Rule #3: Never sell raw stones.
Stones from smelting are the foundation of your byproduct economy. At minimum, crush them into dust ($25). Better: concrete bricks ($360). Best: run through prospectors first for free gem chances, then concrete bricks.
⚠️ The Blast Furnace is a trap!
It costs $25,000 but makes bars 10% LESS valuable than the ore (×0.9 vs Ore Smelter's ×1.2). The only reason it exists is if you want more stone byproducts from already-smelted ores that need re-processing, but in practice the Ore Smelter is strictly better.
💰 Concrete Bricks are the mid-game MVP.
From Stage 5 onwards, your stone byproducts generate more value as concrete bricks ($360 each from 2 free stones) than most early-game main products. This single upgrade (Cement Mixer, $10K) more than doubles your per-ore income.
🔗 Why Plates → Pipes → More?
The Plate Stamper seems worse than the Coiler at first (same +$20, costs more). But plates unlock TWO further processing steps: Pipes (+$20 more), Mech Parts (+$30 more), and Filigree (+20% value). Coils only feed into Circuits and Rings. Invest in the plate path — it has more branches.
🔬 Alloy Stacking (Advanced):
If the game allows alloying alloy bars together, you can recursively merge for compounding 20% bonuses: 2 bars → alloy (×1.2) → 2 alloys → super-alloy (×1.2 again). After N layers with 2N bars: total bonus = 1.2N. With 8 bars (3 layers): 72.8% more value vs separate bars!
📊 The "Per Ore" Metric:
When comparing products, always look at value per ore consumed, not total product value. A $9,000 Power Core using 11 ores ($818/ore) could be worse than a $2,800 Tablet using 5 ores ($560/ore) if you have limited ores. But with byproducts included, Power Core wins: $999/ore vs $658/ore.
