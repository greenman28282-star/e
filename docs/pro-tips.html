🏆 Pro Tips & Quick Reference

Essential rules, key numbers, and optimization insights for experienced players.

⭐ The Golden Rules

Rule 0: Always Prospect Stones

Run every stone through all 5 gem prospectors. Expected value: $141.03 per stone. This is more valuable than any stone processing path. Never skip prospecting.

Rule 1: Clean + Polish Before Smelting

Ore Cleaner (+$10) and Polisher (+$10) add flat bonuses to ore value. These bonuses carry through the Ore Smelter's ×1.2 multiplier. Always upgrade ores before smelting.

Order: Mine → Clean → Polish → Smelt. The +$20 raw becomes +$24 after ×1.2 smelting.

Rule 2: QA Last

The QA Machine applies +20% to item value. As a percentage-based multiplier, it gives the most when applied as the final step after all flat bonuses and other processing.

Rule 3: Never Sell Raw Stones

Raw stones have minimal value. Even basic processing (crush → brick → kiln = $30) is a massive improvement. With full processing, stones are worth $48–$78 each, plus $141 EV from gem prospecting.

⚠️ Critical: Stone Production Rate

The Ore Smelter produces 1 stone per 2 ores (not 1 per 1). This means your stone processing line handles half the throughput of your ore line. Plan your factory layout accordingly — the stone side needs half the capacity of the ore side.

💎 Diamond Prospector Payback

The Diamond Prospector costs $30,000 and contributes $86.08 EV per stone (after polish + cut).

Payback period: $30,000 ÷ $86.08 = ~349 stones

With Ore Smelter (1 stone per 2 ores): ~698 ores to break even.

At Unloader Lv 3 (108 items/min), that's under 7 minutes of play. Extremely fast ROI.

📐 Per-Ore Metric Explained

What is "per-ore value"?

The per-ore metric captures the total value generated from a single ore, including:

1. Bar value: The ore's processed bar (cleaned, polished, smelted, tempered, etc.)

2. Stone byproduct value: Half a stone per ore (Ore Smelter rate), including gem EV and stone processing

3. Downstream product value: The multiplier gained from combining bars into end-game products

When comparing strategies, always think in per-ore terms. A machine that doubles output on 10% of items may be worse than one that adds $5 to every item.

🎯 Quick Decision Guide

Should I buy this machine?

Yes if: It's on the critical path (ore → bar → product) or enables a new recipe chain you'll use.

No if: It's a Blast Furnace (trap), or a Prismatic Crucible (only ×1.15 for 2 gems).

Should I upgrade unloader?

Yes if: Your machines are often idle (waiting for items). More throughput = more money.

No if: Items are backing up at machines. Fix processing bottlenecks first.

What to buy next?

Priority: Ore Cleaner → Polisher → Ore Smelter → Crusher → Brick Mold → Kiln → Prospectors → Coiler/Plate Stamper → Tempering Forge → Combination machines → End-game

Blast Furnace vs Ore Smelter?

Always Ore Smelter. ×1.2 bars + fewer stones beats ×0.9 bars + more stones. The bar value loss is never worth the extra stones.

🚫 Common Mistakes

❌ Selling raw stones

Stones are worth almost nothing raw but $141+ with prospecting. Always process them.

❌ Smelting before cleaning/polishing

Flat bonuses before multipliers = more value. Always clean and polish ores first.

❌ QA before other upgrades

QA is +20%. Apply it last after all other processing for maximum effect.

❌ Cutting gems before polishing

Polish (+$10) before cut (×1.4). Wrong order loses $4 per gem.

❌ Using Blast Furnace as primary smelter

×0.9 bars lose 25% value vs ×1.2 from Ore Smelter. Extra stones don't compensate.

❌ Ignoring stone byproducts

Stone processing adds ~$100+ per ore in byproduct value. It's a huge portion of total income.

Surviving stones → Pair for Concrete Bricks ($100) → Kiln (×1.2) → Polish (+$10) → QA (+20%) → Sell

Remember

Flat bonuses first (Clean, Polish, Tuner) → Multiplicative bonuses (Smelt, Temper, Cut) → Combination (Frame, Casing, etc.) → QA last
