Gem Processing Chain

Optimal Processing Order

1. Prospect stone → Gem found

2. Polish gem (+$10)

3. Cut gem (×1.4)

4. Use in product OR sell directly

⚠️ Don't cut before polishing! Cutting first gives (Base × 1.4) + $10. Polishing first gives (Base + $10) × 1.4. The difference is $4 per gem (×1.4 × $10 − $10 = $4). Small but consistent.

Prospecting Priority

🏆 Always prospect stones before any other stone processing. The expected $141.03/stone from gem prospecting far exceeds the value of converting stones to bricks, glass, or other stone products directly. Process surviving stones (77.38%) through your stone processing chain after prospecting.

Prospector Order Doesn't Matter (Much)

Mathematically, the order you place prospectors doesn't change total EV. However, placing the cheapest gems first means you "use up" stones on lower-value gems first, saving higher-value checks for remaining stones. The conventional order (Topaz → Emerald → Sapphire → Ruby → Diamond) works fine.

Rare Gems (Poudretteite and Beyond)

Gems rarer than Diamond (Poudretteite, Zultanite, Grandidierite, Musgravite, Painite) come from the Ore Upgrader prestige machine or special mechanics. They follow the same Polish → Cut processing chain but have dramatically higher base values, making them extremely lucrative when found.
